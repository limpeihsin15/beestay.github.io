What materials can I recycle ?

Metal

Aluminium cans are 100% recyclable and is able to be returned to the shelf, as a new can. Not only that, metal food and drink cans made from aluminium or steel are also recyclable. Before recycling, the cans and foil must be clean.

Aluminium is one of the most recyclable metals in Malaysia. Besides, stainless steel is the next popular metal.

Paper/Card Board

Almost every paper product is recyclable such as old newspaper, magazines, paperboard, cardboard and etc. However, there are some kinds of paper should not be recycled.

The following are examples of paper that could not be recycle:

  • Soiled Paper
  • Shredded Paper
  • Wax, Plastic or Foil-Coated Paper
  • Paper cups

Cartons, boxes, office printouts and computer forms are the recyclables accepted by Malaysia.

Plastic

Each plastic product has a Plastic Identification Code — a triangle with a number from 1 to 7 inside — usually on the bottom. The code number does not mean the plastic can be recycled. It is simply a way to identify the resin, or plastic type.

Cups, tubs(yogurt or cottage cheese containers), caps, jars, lids, pumps, clamshells, or plastic bags are not recyclable.

PET plastic is the most common material used for single-use bottled beverages, because it is inexpensive, lightweight, unbreakable and easy to recycle.

Recycling Plastic in Japan

Japan is one of the first countries to get into recyclable and biodegradable plastics in a big way. PET (polyethylene terephthalate resin) bottles are collected are turned into high-quality polyester or new bottles. Asahi Breweries has dressed some of its employees in uniforms made from recycled plastic bottles.

Glass

Glass is 100% recyclable and can be recycled endlessly without loss in quality or purity. Most household glass can be recycled and over again.

For example:

  • Food containers or jars
  • Soft drink and beer bottles
  • Wine and liquor bottles

Not all glass can be recycled. Some glass items, such as car windshields, cooking dishes, broken or sharp glass and light bulbs, aren't usually accepted by local recycling systems.

⚠ A small notes for you

Do you know Germany has the best recycling rate in the world? Germany produces 30 million tons of garbagae anually and it manages to recycle 56.1% in 2018. Germany have six different bins to help them separate the materials: black for general waste, blue for paper, yellow for plastic, white for clear glass, green for colored glass and brown for composting.
